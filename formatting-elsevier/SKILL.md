# formatting-elsevier

## Workflow

1. Confirm the exact Elsevier journal and article type.
2. Open the journal-specific Guide for Authors. Elsevier requirements are journal-specific, and generic Elsevier pages are only secondary.
3. Check whether highlights, graphical abstract, separate title page, declaration forms, or research data statements are required.
4. Audit structure, references, figures, tables, supplementary material, and declarations.
5. Produce the requested manuscript edits or submission package.

## Required Inputs

Ask for:

- Target Elsevier journal.
- Article type.
- Guide for Authors URL.
- Manuscript file or text.
- Reference style, if known.
- Whether there are humans, animals, clinical trials, public databases, AI tools, research data, or supplementary materials.

## Checks

Check:

- Title page, author details, affiliations, corresponding author, and present/permanent addresses if relevant.
- Abstract type, highlights, graphical abstract, keywords, abbreviations, and main text structure.
- Figure and table files, captions, permissions, and citation order.
- References and in-text citations using the target journal style.
- Declaration of interest, funding, ethics, consent, data availability, author contributions, acknowledgments, and AI-assisted writing disclosure when required.

## Output

Use "required by Guide for Authors", "recommended by Elsevier", and "not confirmed" labels to avoid mixing journal-specific and publisher-wide requirements.

## Guardrails

Do not assume highlights, graphical abstracts, reference style, or word limits apply until confirmed on the target journal page.
