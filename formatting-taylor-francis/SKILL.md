# Taylor & Francis Formatting

## Workflow

1. Confirm the exact Taylor & Francis or Routledge journal and article type.
2. Read the journal's Instructions for Authors. Use `references/official-guidelines.md` as a source map.
3. Determine whether the journal allows format-free initial submission or requires strict formatting.
4. Audit structure, references, figures, tables, supplementary files, declarations, and reporting guidelines.
5. Produce the requested checklist or manuscript edits.

## Required Inputs

Ask for:

- Target journal.
- Article type.
- Instructions for Authors URL.
- Manuscript file or text.
- Reference style, if known.
- Human/animal/public data/clinical trial/AI/supplementary material status.

## Checks

Check:

- Title page, authorship, affiliations, acknowledgments, and disclosure statement.
- Abstract type and keywords.
- Main headings required by the article type.
- Reference style and in-text citation format.
- Figures, tables, captions, permissions, and supplementary material.
- Ethics, consent, data availability, funding, conflicts of interest or disclosure, and reporting guideline compliance.

## Guardrails

Do not infer word limits, abstract type, reference style, or file preparation rules from another Taylor & Francis journal. Always verify the target title.
