# PLOS Formatting

## Workflow

1. Confirm the exact PLOS journal and article type.
2. Read the current journal-specific PLOS submission guidelines. Use `references/official-guidelines.md` as a source map.
3. Check article-type-specific requirements, including any author summary, data availability, reporting guideline, or file requirement.
4. Audit manuscript structure, references, figures, tables, supporting information, and declarations.
5. Produce the requested checklist, revised manuscript sections, or submission package.

## Required Inputs

Ask for:

- Target PLOS journal.
- Article type.
- Submission guidelines URL.
- Manuscript file or text.
- Whether humans, animals, field studies, clinical trials, public data, code, AI tools, or supporting information are involved.

## Checks

Check:

- Title, authors, affiliations, abstract, and any journal-specific author summary.
- Main text structure.
- Data availability statement and repository/accession details.
- Funding statement and competing interests.
- Ethics approval, consent, permissions, and trial registration when applicable.
- References, figures, tables, captions, and supporting information.
- Reporting guidelines and checklist requirements.

## Guardrails

PLOS places strong emphasis on data availability and ethical transparency. Do not invent repository links, accession numbers, permissions, or ethics details; mark them missing and ask for confirmation.
