# Metabolites Official Guidelines

Last checked: 2026-07-15.

Use current official sources as the final authority:

- Instructions for Authors: https://www.mdpi.com/journal/metabolites/instructions
- Standard Word template: https://mdpi-res.com/files/word-templates/metabolites-template.dot
- MDPI layout guide: https://www.mdpi.com/authors/layout
- MDPI reference guide: https://www.mdpi.com/authors/references

The template URLs are unversioned or date-stamped resources that may change. Resolve links from the live instructions page and record the final URL, retrieval date, article type, and SHA-256 for each run.

## Template Selection

Select the article type before downloading a template:

- Article, Original Research, Review, or Systematic Review: use the standard Word template linked above.
- Protocol: resolve the current Protocol template from the live instructions. The link checked on 2026-07-15 was `https://res.mdpi.com/data/metabolites-template-v2-20260622.dot`.
- Data Descriptor: resolve the current Data Descriptor template from the live instructions. The link checked on 2026-07-15 was `https://res.mdpi.com/data/metabolites-template-v3-20260622.dot`.

Do not invent a template URL from the journal slug. If the template cannot be obtained, ask for an upload and label the result structure-only.
The live page currently resolves the standard template through `mdpi-res.com` and the dated specialized templates through `res.mdpi.com`; preserve the resolved URL instead of normalizing one host to the other.

## Research Article Structure

Use this order for a standard research article:

1. Abstract
2. Keywords
3. Introduction
4. Materials and Methods
5. Results
6. Discussion
7. Conclusions, when useful

Results and Discussion may be combined. Conclusions are optional and must not be added merely to satisfy a generic IMRaD checklist.

## Abstract And Keywords

- Use an approximately 250-word structured abstract for original research and systematic reviews.
- Use the inline labels `Background/Objectives`, `Methods`, `Results`, and `Conclusions`.
- Treat the word target as a warning, not a hard failure.
- Provide 3–10 keywords separated with semicolons in the Word template.

## Authors

- Use full author names and complete affiliation addresses, including city, postal code, state or province when relevant, and country.
- Identify at least one corresponding author with `*` and a `* Correspondence:` line.
- Identify equal contributors with `†`, add “These authors contributed equally to this work”, and reflect equal roles in the CRediT statement.
- Never infer missing affiliation or correspondence data.

## Back Matter

Process back matter in this order when applicable:

1. Supplementary Materials
2. Author Contributions using CRediT roles
3. Funding, including grant numbers and sponsor role where relevant
4. Institutional Review Board Statement
5. Informed Consent Statement
6. Data Availability Statement
7. Acknowledgments
8. Conflicts of Interest
9. Optional abbreviations or appendices
10. References

Data Availability is required for every article. For human or animal work, record the committee name, protocol code, and approval date. Human-participant work also requires consent or a factually supported waiver statement.

Use “The authors declare no conflict of interest.” only when the authors confirm it is true. For industry funding, disclose the sponsor's role in design, conduct, interpretation, and writing.

Disclose generative AI in Materials and Methods when it materially generated or altered research text, data, figures, design, or analysis. Pure spelling, grammar, punctuation, or formatting assistance does not require a declaration under the policy checked on 2026-07-15.

## Figures And Tables

- Prefer PNG, JPEG, or TIFF figures at 600 dpi or higher; treat lower effective resolution as an author warning.
- Place figures near first citation, number them in order, keep aspect ratio, and do not synthetically upscale.
- Put figure captions below figures and table captions above tables.
- Combine multi-panel figures into one file and explain every panel and symbol.
- Keep tables editable in Word, repeat header rows, fit the printable width, and keep table text at 8 pt or larger.

## Citations And References

- Number references by first appearance, including citations in figure and table captions.
- Use square brackets before punctuation, such as `[1]`, `[1–3]`, and `[1,3]`.
- Use the MDPI ACS pattern: authors separated by semicolons; full article title; verified ISO 4 journal abbreviation; year, volume, and pages or article number.
- Preserve Zotero or EndNote fields when present. Do not flatten fields during format-only work.
- Never invent DOI, issue, pages, accessed date, or journal abbreviations.

## Optional Elements And Submission Files

- Highlights are optional. If supplied, use “Main findings” and “Implications”, with no more than two bullets per part.
- A graphical abstract is optional where suitable.
- A cover letter is required by the instructions checked on 2026-07-15.
- Apply the reporting checklist appropriate to the study design.
