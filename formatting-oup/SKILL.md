# OUP Formatting

## Workflow

1. Confirm the exact Oxford University Press or Oxford Academic journal and article type.
2. Read the journal-specific Instructions to Authors. Use `references/official-guidelines.md` as a source map for common OUP authoring resources.
3. Check journal-specific reference style, word limits, figure/table requirements, and declaration sections.
4. Audit manuscript structure and submission files.
5. Produce the requested checklist, revised sections, or package.

## Required Inputs

Ask for:

- Target OUP journal.
- Article type.
- Instructions to Authors URL.
- Manuscript file or text.
- Reference style, if known.
- Human/animal/public data/clinical trial/AI/supplementary material status.

## Checks

Check:

- Title page, authorship, affiliations, corresponding author, and ORCID requirements.
- Abstract, keywords, headings, and article-type limits.
- Figures, tables, captions, permissions, and supplementary data.
- References and in-text citations.
- Ethics, consent, clinical trial registration, data availability, funding, conflict of interest, author contributions, and reporting guideline requirements.

## Guardrails

Do not assume a single OUP house style for all journals. Use the target journal page for final decisions.
